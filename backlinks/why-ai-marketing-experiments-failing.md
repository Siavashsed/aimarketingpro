## QUORA
**Answer this question:** Why are my AI marketing experiments not producing good results?

This is one of the most common problems I see in performance marketing right now. Most teams assume the AI tool is the bottleneck when the real issue is almost always the experimental setup.

I learned this the hard way running a mid-funnel email sequence test for a SaaS client. We had a $40k/month ad budget, decent volume flowing through the funnel, and decided to use AI for copy generation. Seemed like an obvious win. Six weeks later, open rates had completely tanked.

Our diagnosis process revealed three critical failures:

First, we'd given the AI zero audience context - no historical performance data, no segment definitions, no understanding of what messaging actually converted at that funnel stage for *this specific audience*.

Second, we had no baseline metrics. We couldn't articulate what "success" actually looked like beyond "higher open rates."

Third, we'd removed human judgment entirely. We weren't A/B testing variations or monitoring for qualitative signals that the copy wasn't resonating.

When we rebuilt with proper audience segmentation, clear success criteria, and hybrid human-AI workflow, the same tools produced exceptional results.

The AI didn't improve. Our experimental rigor did. This is true across nearly every failing AI marketing experiment I've analyzed. The technology works - your framework needs work.
