**Answer this question:** How did Google's Search Generative Experience (SGE) impact SEO and content marketing strategies in 2023?

When SGE rolled out to U.S. users in May 2023, it fundamentally changed how search results were presented, and I think the initial reactions—both panic and dismissal—missed the actual shift happening.

The most immediate visible change was that featured snippets and traditional SERP real estate got absorbed into AI-generated summary boxes. For teams running paid and organic strategy simultaneously, this looked catastrophic at first. You lose prime positioning, right? But that's where most people stopped analyzing.

What I observed across multiple accounts was that traffic didn't disappear—it adapted. Yes, fewer people stayed on the SERP reading your snippet. But more people clicked through to the full article because the AI summary created curiosity gaps instead of providing complete answers. The summary would touch on your key points but not exhaustively.

The real strategic shift was this: stop optimizing purely for snippet capture and start optimizing for being the most valuable expansion of what the AI summary introduces. Your content needed to go deeper, provide nuance, offer data or frameworks that a summary can't deliver in a few lines.

Industry and query type mattered too. Transactional queries handled differently than informational ones. Some verticals saw immediate impact; others adapted more gradually.
