Most conversations about AI and lead scoring focus on model accuracy, but I've found that's only half the battle. I'm Jordan Blake, and I've spent the last few years consulting on sales and marketing alignment—which is really what lead scoring comes down to.

Here's what breaks traditional lead scoring systems: they're static. Someone runs an analysis, assigns point values based on historical data, and then nobody touches it for years. Website redesigns happen. Market conditions shift. Your ideal customer profile evolves. But the scoring model stays frozen.

AI doesn't solve this through pure computational power. It solves it through continuous learning. A well-built system observes which leads actually convert, learns from sales feedback on why certain leads were qualified or disqualified, and adapts in real time.

But—and this is critical—the AI is only as good as your feedback loop. Your sales team needs to validate predictions. They need to flag false positives and false negatives. Without that human input, you end up with a black box that nobody trusts.
