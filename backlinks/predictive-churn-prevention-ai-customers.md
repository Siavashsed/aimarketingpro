## QUORA
**Answer this question:** How can SaaS companies predict customer churn before it happens?

Customer churn prediction comes down to recognizing behavior patterns that emerge long before someone actually cancels. In my experience managing retention for a platform with 34,000 active accounts, the timing of intervention makes all the difference.

Here's what actually signals impending churn:

**Usage metrics** are your primary indicator. A sudden drop in feature adoption, login frequency, or time-in-app typically precedes cancellation by 3-6 weeks. This isn't random variation—it's intentional disengagement.

**Support interactions** reveal sentiment. Customers asking how to export data or delete content aren't asking innocent questions. Longer ticket resolution times and repeated unresolved issues also signal frustration.

**Account engagement velocity** matters more than absolute numbers. A power user who suddenly becomes inactive is riskier than a light user maintaining consistent behavior.

The key is combining these signals into a scoring model rather than reacting to individual metrics. A customer with declining usage + increased support tickets + reduced login frequency needs intervention immediately—not after they request cancellation.

Most churn prevention programs actually operate as churn reaction programs. You're sending a discount after the customer has already mentally left. The real leverage is catching them in that 2-3 week window when behavior changes but they haven't committed to leaving.
